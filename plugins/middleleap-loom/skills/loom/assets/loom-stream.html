Run · the third arc

The loop closes — Run feeds Discovery

The double diamond is not a line with two ends; it is a loop. Past the delivery seam the cloth runs, and what running learns is fed back into Discovery as evidence. What the bundle ships is the feedback machinery — the wire — not the system it carries. It does not run your service, page your on-call, or detect your incidents.

Seam in (⑦/⑧). Run begins at delivery steps ⑦ Deploy and ⑧ Evidence: merge auto-deploys to a live target, a smoke suite runs against the live URL, and the release seals its evidence bundle. Past that seam the warp persists — four-eyes, INSERT-only audit, lineage, the sealed trail keep accreting as the system operates.

What operates in Run. The run-time expression of the warp is Continuous Assurance — the watch→assess→check→test→evidence→confirm lifecycle, re-run on a schedule and on events. Two of its agents ship today: ① Watch (change-watch) catches drift / CVE / regulatory horizon items, and ② Assess (risk-reviewer) produces the ROUTE verdict. The bundle listens; it does not run the pager.

The feedback edge — a Run signal is just a signal

Discovery is evidence-gated (D2), so a production signal re-enters through the same doors any evidence uses — never onto the floor.

The routing triage

Most signals do not reopen Discovery. The triage is the ROUTE verdict ② Assess already produces, and the operations-signal gate (operations-signal-check.mjs) is the Run→Discovery analogue of the waist gate: it makes triage + traceability the exit condition for a signal. A signal with no route is the one failure it exists to catch.

The operations-signal gate reads a JSON log — it enforces that a logged signal is triaged and traceable. It does not detect incidents, watch SLOs, or read production; detection is the adopter's operation. The bundle ships the discipline that a signal, once produced, cannot fall on the floor — and nothing more.

The rule that keeps this honest

A scanner that reports is hygiene; the control is the CI gate that blocks merge on a policy violation, wired so the agent identity cannot bypass it (branch protection / protected CI — HG-0001, HG-0002). Buying the tools is not the control.

And the caveats the Loom would insist on: "zero-CVE" means known CVEs at build time, not proof of no vulnerabilities; SCA covers declared dependencies, not your custom code — that is SAST's job; tool choice stays the institution's. These are recommended instances, not a mandate.
